Here's a timeline of Omar Khayyam's life:

  • 1048 - Omar Khayyam is born in Nishapur, Persia (modern-day Iran).
  • 1070 - Begins his studies in mathematics and astronomy.
  • 1074 - Publishes "The Treatise on Demonstration of Problems of Algebra," showcasing his mathematical prowess.
  • 1079 - Works on calendar reforms and develops the Jalali calendar, known for its accuracy.
  • 1092 - Begins his travels, visiting various cities and engaging in intellectual exchanges.
  • 1110 - Omar Khayyam completes his renowned work, the "Rubaiyat," a collection of quatrains exploring themes of love, mortality, and the nature of existence.
  • 1123 - Omar Khayyam passes away, leaving behind a legacy of intellectual achievements and poetic brilliance.
